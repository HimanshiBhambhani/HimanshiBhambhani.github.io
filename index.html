// about

From resolving live incidents to shaping what gets built.

I started in engineering — writing Java & PL/SQL for Oracle Agile PLM at Cisco, then owning enterprise SD-WAN and Meraki deployments as a customer-facing technical engineer. Those years taught me something most PMs learn late: what it actually feels like to be on the other end of a broken product.

Resolving live enterprise incidents turned raw user pain into a habit of root-cause thinking — and converting those pain points into shipped process improvements gave me a taste for owning outcomes, not just tickets.

Today I channel that into data-driven product work: user research, problem framing, PRDs, KPI trees, and A/B experiments — backed by the technical fluency to build the thing myself. My four recent projects span a consumer UX redesign, two full-stack AI products, and an MCP-based insights tool — each taken from problem definition to a working prototype or deployment.
